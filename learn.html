Communication Fundamentals

Master the building blocks of effective interaction.

Verbal Communication

1. Active Listening

Focus entirely on the speaker. Avoid thinking about your response while they are talking. Use verbal cues like "I see" or "Tell me more."

2. Clarity & Conciseness

Get straight to the point. Use simple language and avoid filler words like "um," "uh," and "like."

3. The Power of Tone

How you say something is often more important than what you say. Practice speaking with confidence and empathy.

4. Asking Questions

Use open-ended questions (Who, What, Where, When, Why, How) to encourage deeper conversation and show interest.

Non-Verbal Communication

1. Body Posture

Stand or sit tall to project confidence. Keep your arms uncrossed to appear open and approachable.

2. Eye Contact

Maintain steady, comfortable eye contact (3-5 seconds) to build trust and show engagement.

3. Hand Gestures

Use your hands to emphasize points, but avoid fidgeting, which can signal nervousness.

4. Facial Expressions

Ensure your face matches your message. A warm smile can break the ice in almost any situation.

MKVK Pro Tips

Tip #1: Before an important conversation, take three deep breaths. It calms your nervous system and helps you speak more clearly.

Tip #2: Record yourself speaking for 2 minutes once a week. You'll notice habits (good and bad) that you never knew you had!
